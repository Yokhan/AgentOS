# Russian Business Correspondence

This profile is a child of `writing.md` and `russian-writing-profile.md`. Load it
for Russian work email, client messages, task assignment, status updates,
meeting summaries, feedback, claims, refusals, support replies, and escalation.

The method is derived from public, attributable material for:

- Maxim Ilyakhov and Lyudmila Sarycheva, `Новые правила деловой переписки`;
- Maxim Ilyakhov, `Текст по полочкам`;
- Maxim Ilyakhov, the open electronic guide `Как написать`;
- project-owned correspondence, policies, and terminology when registered.

The rules and examples below are a template-authored operational synthesis. They
are not quotations, reconstructed book pages, or permission to imitate an
author's voice.

## Correspondence Contract

Before writing, determine:

1. Sender, recipient, relationship, authority, and prior state.
2. The one state change the message must produce now.
3. Facts, attachments, links, decisions, and unavailable information.
4. Recipient action, owner, timing, consequence, and right to decline or object.
5. Channel, privacy, copied recipients, permanence, and escalation path.
6. Tone appropriate to consequence, not a stock level of formality.

If authority, promise, deadline, legal position, money, blame, or public exposure
is unclear, stop and ask. Polished wording must not conceal an unresolved
decision.

## Core Rules

### 1. Respect And Care Are Actions

Respect means preserving the recipient's agency, time, dignity, and relevant
context. Care means doing the preparatory work that makes the next action easy.
Greetings, thanks, and formal closings are optional social signals; they cannot
compensate for hidden work, manipulation, or missing information.

### 2. Put The Useful State First

Start with the decision, change, request, problem, or result that matters to the
recipient. Add background after the recipient can orient themselves. A subject
line should identify the work item or decision, not merely say `Вопрос`,
`Срочно`, or `Информация`.

### 3. Make The Action Executable

State what is needed, from whom, in what form, by when, and how completion will
be recognized. Include the files, links, draft, options, or prefilled material
the recipient needs. Explain a deadline when its reason changes priorities.

### 4. Preserve The Right To Respond Honestly

Do not manufacture consent with `заранее спасибо`, fake urgency, copied
executives, or a request framed as an accomplished decision. When refusal is
possible, say so and give a useful response path. When the action is mandatory,
name the actual authority or dependency instead of pretending it is a favor.

### 5. Separate People From Work

Describe the artifact, observed behavior, consequence, and required change. Do
not diagnose character, intelligence, motivation, or professionalism. Give
unrequested criticism privately and first establish that the recipient wants or
is required to receive it.

### 6. Critique Through Consequences

Use the shape `if we keep X, Y happens; changing X to Z protects Q`. Ask about
the task before prescribing a solution outside your ownership. Criticism should
improve the shared result, not demonstrate the sender's status.

### 7. Report Decisions, Not Meeting Atmosphere

A meeting summary records decisions, owners, dates, unresolved questions, and
the next checkpoint. It does not reproduce the conversation in discovery order.
Invite corrections by a concrete time when silence will be treated as agreement.

### 8. Report Work Through Results And Drift

Lead with the current result. Then state completed work, evidence, deviation from
plan, cause, impact, next action, and any decision required from the recipient.
Do not hide blocked work behind activity lists.

### 9. Deliver Bad News Early

State the refusal, failure, delay, or limit before a long justification. Explain
only relevant causes, own the next action, offer real alternatives, and name the
next update. Do not dilute bad news until the recipient can no longer find it.

### 10. Make Claims Actionable

For a complaint or claim, record the expected state, observed state, evidence,
impact, requested remedy, owner, deadline, and next escalation step. Avoid insult,
speculation about intent, and threats that the sender cannot or will not carry
out.

### 11. Do Not Fake Personal Communication

Mass messages should not imitate a personal letter. Put the useful event, terms,
date, place, and action together; make subscription status and opt-out visible
where applicable. Personalization must come from real recipient context.

### 12. Use Copies Deliberately

Every copied recipient needs a reason: decision, awareness, execution, or
accountability. Do not use `CC` as intimidation. Do not expose private criticism,
personal data, commercial terms, or incident details beyond their audience.

### 13. Keep Related Actions Together

One message may contain several actions when they belong to one decision and
share context. Separate unrelated requests so owners and deadlines remain
visible. Use a compact table or list for multiple owners; do not bury a task in a
paragraph.

### 14. Follow Up With New Value

A follow-up restates the decision or request, shows what changed, and makes the
next response easy. `Напоминаю о себе` without context transfers reconstruction
work to the recipient.

### 15. Match Tone To Consequence

Warmth, brevity, formality, and directness depend on relationship, risk, culture,
and channel. Do not use casual friendliness to mask coercion or bureaucratic
language to avoid responsibility. In conflict, lower emotional temperature while
keeping facts and boundaries explicit.

## Genre Contracts

| Genre | Minimum complete content |
| --- | --- |
| Request | result needed, why this recipient, prepared inputs, deadline/reason, response options |
| Task | outcome, owner, inputs, constraints, quality bar, due date, verification |
| Status | current result, evidence, drift, impact, next step, requested decision |
| Meeting summary | decisions, owners, dates, open questions, correction window |
| Feedback | task context, observation, consequence, proposed change, discussion path |
| Refusal | clear answer, relevant reason, available alternative or boundary |
| Claim | expected/observed state, evidence, impact, remedy, deadline, escalation |
| Incident update | affected function, known/unknown, workaround, owner, next update |
| Commercial proposal | recipient situation, useful outcome, mechanism, evidence, terms, next action |
| Mass message | event/change, audience impact, terms, date/place, action, opt-out when required |

## Original Examples

Each stronger version is valid only when its facts are true.

### Request

**Weak:** `Коллеги, срочно посмотрите договор. Заранее спасибо!`

**Diagnosis:** no owner, decision, material, deadline reason, or honest response
option; gratitude is used as pressure.

**Stronger:** `Анна, проверьте пункты 4 и 7 в договоре до четверга 12:00. В 15:00
отправляем версию поставщику. Нужен ответ: согласовать или перечислить риски.
Файл и комментарии юриста приложены. Если срок не помещается, напишите сегодня
до 17:00 — перенесём отправку или подключим Олега.`

### Task

**Weak:** `Надо улучшить онбординг к пятнице.`

**Diagnosis:** activity replaces outcome, scope and acceptance are absent.

**Stronger:** `К пятнице пользователь должен зарегистрироваться, подтвердить
почту и создать первый проект без помощи поддержки. Марина отвечает за макеты,
Игорь — за события аналитики. Проверяем на тестовом аккаунте по сценарию в Jira.`

### Status And Drift

**Weak:** `Работаем над интеграцией, почти закончили.`

**Diagnosis:** motion is reported instead of product state and remaining risk.

**Stronger:** `Импорт заказов работает на тестовом магазине. Возвраты пока не
передаются: API партнёра не возвращает причину. Сегодня проверим резервный метод;
в 16:00 сообщу, сохраняется ли релиз во вторник.`

### Meeting Summary

**Weak:** `Обсудили запуск и разные варианты. Вроде решили идти по плану.`

**Diagnosis:** no decision, owner, date, or open issue.

**Stronger:** `Решили открыть доступ 24 июля для 10% клиентов. Ольга готовит
список до 22 июля, Павел включает флаг. Открытый вопрос — лимит экспорта; решение
принимает Анна 21 июля. Поправьте итоги до завтра 11:00.`

### Feedback

**Weak:** `Ты невнимательно сделал отчёт и опять всё перепутал.`

**Diagnosis:** the message judges a person and gives no repair path.

**Stronger:** `В отчёте расходы за июнь попали в колонку мая, поэтому итог меньше
на 240 тысяч рублей. Перенеси строки 18-31 и повторно сверь сумму с выгрузкой.
Если источник размечен неоднозначно, разберём его вместе.`

### Refusal

**Weak:** `К сожалению, в настоящее время не представляется возможным выполнить
вашу просьбу.`

**Diagnosis:** the answer is hidden and the recipient has no next option.

**Stronger:** `Не сможем выпустить интеграцию в июле: до 28 июля команда закрывает
ошибку с двойными списаниями. Можем поставить интеграцию первой задачей августа
или передать спецификацию вашей команде.`

### Claim

**Weak:** `Ваш сервис отвратительный. Если немедленно не исправите, пожалеете.`

**Diagnosis:** no verifiable event, remedy, deadline, or credible escalation.

**Stronger:** `Счёт 4187 оплачен 14 июля, но доступ всё ещё закрыт. Платёжное
поручение приложено. Восстановите доступ или сообщите причину до 18 июля 12:00.
Если ответа не будет, направим претензию по адресу из договора.`

### Client Incident

**Weak:** `Приносим искренние извинения за доставленные неудобства. Специалисты
уже занимаются проблемой.`

**Diagnosis:** ritual empathy replaces affected function, workaround, and update.

**Stronger:** `С 10:40 не загружаются новые документы. Уже загруженные файлы
доступны; срочный документ можно отправить через форму поддержки. Причину ищет
дежурная команда. Следующее обновление — в 12:00.`

## Acceptance Gate

- Can the recipient identify the message's state change from the first screen?
- Is every requested action owned, bounded, and executable?
- Are deadlines real, explained when material, and free of fake urgency?
- Does the message preserve agency without disguising mandatory authority?
- Are criticism and claims about work and consequences rather than personality?
- Are decisions, drift, uncertainty, and next updates explicit?
- Are copied recipients and disclosed information necessary?
- Would removing ritual politeness reveal coercion, missing work, or missing facts?
